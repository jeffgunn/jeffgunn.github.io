Coral Gardens, Snorkeling and Sting Ray Adventure

Dates: Thursday, May 16, 2019 or Saturday, May 18, 2019
Time: 9:00 am – 12:00 pm
Duration: 3 hours
Attire: Please wear your swimsuit and sneakers or reef shoes, also recommended to bring sunscreen, hat, sunglasses and a t-shirt.

Explore the exquisite beauty and undersea life of Bora Bora’s lagoon during this stingray ballet and snorkelling safari. Depart the Four Seasons Resort and cruise by the southern tip of the Island, past Mt. Otemanu and Matira Beach. You will then drop anchor on a shallow sandbank to experience the elegant harmless eagle rays and bat rays. You will watch in amazement as your guide hand-feeds these gentle creatures, as they glide effortlessly around you. Next, you will continue on for two additional stops for snorkeling and viewing at a pristine coral garden and outside the lagoon. Along the way, you’ll see an abundance of multi-colored, tropical fish, octopus, eel, and the more dramatic black tipped reef sharks.

Stingrays are known as the most graceful animals of the sea, these enormous creatures will glide with elegance past your boat again and again. Their fluid movement will make you think they are flying under water. You may be lucky to see a Manta-Ray if conditions and mother-nature are kind.

Deep Sea Fishing

Date: Thursday, May 16, 2019 or Saturday, May 18, 2019
Time: 8:30 am – 12:30 pm
Duration: 4 hours
Attire: Comfortable shoes or sneakers, UV protected shirt, hat, sunglasses, sunscreen are recommended.

The waters off the coast of Bora Bora in French Polynesia are rich in sea life and great for deep sea sport fishing. Fishing conditions are optimal in May, this is when the water is warmest. French Polynesia is one of the few places in the world where you can catch Pacific Blue Marlin all year round. Mahi-Mahi, Wahoo, Skipjack Tuna, Trevally, Jacks and Barracuda are also commonly fished during this time. The fishing grounds begin one mile outside the reef, where the water drops off to more than 1,000 metres (3,280 feet). You will fish aboard high end vessels meant for fishing at sea, it’s designed and built exclusively for serious competitors, but novices don’t worry, you can learn just like the experts, all levels are welcome. There are typically 5 fishermen per boat. 

Chef can prepare your catch.

Vaitape Shopping and The Pearl Farm Experience

Date: Thursday, May 16, 2019
Time: 10:00 am – 3:30 pm
Duration: 5 ½ hours
Attire: Comfortable walking shoes, hat and sunglasses

There is no better way to see and understand the Island of Bora Bora than via the Circle Island land excursion. You will depart from the resort by fast boat to Vaitape, the main island of Bora Bora. You will sit back and relax in your air-conditioned mini-coach with your English-speaking driver/guide, while seeing the sites of Vaitape in style. Along the way you will visit various souvenir and artist boutiques. You also pass by the sandy Matira Beach for a look over the vistas of the island.

One of your final stops is at the Bora Bora Pearl Company, where you will learn about Tahitian pearls by the experts, they will share many interesting stories all about the mysteries of the Islands most treasured pearl.  You will then have a hands-on experience at The Farm, where the mysteries of the cultured pearl are further revealed and you will experience from the origin of discovery to when it reveals its brilliance, a true gift from mother nature and truly a rare moment.

You will leave with a special piece of jewelry, you select the design, the mounting and the precious metals that will reflect your personality, in a unique, unduplicated creation as the master jeweler composes your own, peerless original Tahitian pearl jewelry. This unique shopping day will end with lunch at the Bora Bora Yacht Club and then back to Four Seasons by boat transfer.

The Polynesian conviviality testifies of the “joie de vivre” in these islands, and this conviviality implies the the art of the table. Polynesians are gourmets for whom everything deserves to be celebrated by a good “ma’a” (meal). Every event is an occasion to cook and to savour various dishes born from the diversity of the culinary cultures in our islands.  They believe great feasts to enjoy in family, with friends, or with your beloved one. Spend the afternoon with Four Seasons culinary chefs and learn some new techniques and enjoy the fruits of your labor.  Set alongside the beautiful scenery, you will enjoy a relaxed culinary experience.

Marine Biology Experience with Snorkeling and Kayak Experience

Date: Saturday, May 18, 2019
Time:10:30 am – 12:45 pm
Duration: 2 hours and 15 minutes
Attire: Comfortable clothing, walking shoes and sunscreen
Activity Requirements: A minimum comfort level of being in the water with a life jacket is required if you plan on swimming and experiencing the stingrays or snorkeling. As an option, you may also remain on the boat.

Nestled within the grounds of the Resort is an inner lagoon, teeming with exotic marine life. The Ruahatu (God of the Ocean) Lagoon Sanctuary at Four Seasons Resort Bora Bora is more than just a spectacular snorkeling location, but also a research facility and a place where the marine environment not only thrives but grows, furthering the delicate and wondrous ecosystem.

You will spend some time with Denis Schneider, the local marine biologist and expert of all things under the sea.  The sanctuary is home to over 100 colorful species, including octopus, eagle ray, lizardfish, Picasso trumpet fish, soldier fish, fire fish, unicorn fish, spotted puffer fish, parrotfish, peacock damselfish, clownfish and butterfly fish. Sea urchins, anemones and shrimp are also present, along with many other native species of marine life.  Guided snorkeling tours of the lagoon are part of today’s journey with Denis while learning all about the incredible marine environment and you will also learn about coral grafting. This is a truly special and unique opportunity to not only learn about their precious sanctuary but also contribute to it’s ever blossoming beauty.

You will finish up your excursion with a kayak trip around the lagoon to discover more magnificent and beautiful sights.
