If you do not have a passport and are obtaining one for the first time, you must apply in person at a passport acceptance facility such as a post office, public library or municipal office. You will need two photographs of yourself, proof of U.S. citizenship and a valid form of photo identification such as a driver’s license. It can take four to six weeks from the time you apply to receive a new passport. Expedited applications are typically processed in two to three weeks.
For instructions and a directory of passport acceptance facilities, go to www.travel.state.gov and click on “Passports.”

If you need to renew your passport, you may be able to do it by mail. Go to www.travel.state.gov and click on “Passports” for instructions and downloadable forms.

If your name change occurred within one year of the date your passport was issued, you can send a completed U.S. Passport Re-Application Form (available at www.travel.state.gov/passport or a passport acceptance facility), a certified copy of your marriage certificate or your name change court decree, two recent color photographs (2”x 2”) and your current valid passport to:
National Passport Processing
P.O. Box 13290
Philadelphia, PA 19101-3290

There is no fee for this service unless you require it be expedited. Please note: photocopies and notarized copies are not acceptable for passport purposes.

Value Added Tax (VAT)

In Europe, most prices include a value-added tax (VAT) as high as 25% so be sure to inquire about the process when you make a purchase. It is like a sales tax, except it is built into the price you pay instead of being added at the cash register.Â  As a tourist who lives outside the European Union (EU), you may be able to claim a VAT refund in these ways.

  1. The most common way is to use the store's refund affiliate, which can be identified by a decal such as, "Tax Free Shopping" or "Premier Tax Free," in the store window. This is the easiest and most reliable method. The store gives you a tax-free shopping receipt to present to Customs when you leave Europe. After presenting your receipt to the customs official, you will take your stamped receipt to the refund service's airport desk or border kiosk for an immediate refund or, drop them in a special box at the airport for the refund service or mail them to the refund service's nearest office when you get home. You can have refunds credited to your Visa, MasterCard or other credit card in your own currency.
  1. In rare instances, you may get a refund directly from the shop where you make your purchase. Request a VAT refund form, have it stamped by a Customs official when you leave Europe and mail the stamped form back to the store. For smaller transactions, the cost of cashing a foreign currency check may exceed the amount of the refund. However, this method is worth considering for large purchases or if the merchant will credit the refund to your credit card account instead of mailing you a check. In the latter case, your credit card company will automatically convert the refund to your local currency.
