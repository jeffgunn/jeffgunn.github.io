Activities Hosted by Shell

Mauthausen, Austria

Bike Tour with Special Performance
Date: Saturday, May 5, 2018
Time: 9:00 am – 12:00 pm
Duration: 3 hours
Attire: Comfortable clothing and walking shoes
Bike tour includes rental bikes and helmets, ferry to cross the Danube from Mauthausen to St. Florian side, return via motorcoach transfer after a special private performance.

St Florian Monastery Tour with Special Performance
Date: Saturday, May 5, 2018
Time: 9:00 am – 12:00 pm
Duration: 3 hours
Attire: Comfortable clothing and walking shoes
St. Florian Monastery founded in the Carolingian period. From 1071 it has housed a community of Augustinian Canons, and is thus is one of the oldest operational monasteries in the world following the Rule of St. Augustine. You will be amazed by the library with more than 150.000 volumes, the imperial marble hall, the famous Altdorfer Gallery and the splendid church. Return via motorcoach after a special private performance.

Vienna, Austria

Café Demel Cake Decorating Experience
Date: Sunday, May 6, 2018
Time: 9:00am – 11:00 am
Duration: 2 hours
Attire: Casual, comfortable clothing, comfortable walking shoes
Café Demel has always been famous for its amazing sculptures of chocolate and marzipan presented in the windows of the shop. Today the chef de patisserie demonstrates the high art of decorating a cake. You will get a Sacher cake and have the opportunity to decorate any way you like with chef’s input and direction. The best part is enjoying the fruits of your labor, or you can take it to go and enjoy it later in the day.

Vienna City Walk
Date: Sunday, May 6, 2018
Time: 9:30 am – 12:00 pm
Duration: 2 ½ hours
Attire: Comfortable clothing and walking shoes
Vienna is old, Vienna is new – and so varied: from the magnificent baroque buildings to “golden” Art Nouveau to the latest architecture. Stay within the famous Ring Boulevard on your city tour. Drive the Ring Boulevard, then stop behind the State Opera on Albertinaplatz. Continue from here on foot to the Imperial Palace, the winter residence of the Imperial Family, passing elegant boutiques and the well-known Café Demel and proceed to the city center, to St. Stephan’s Cathedral where the tour ends.

Schonbrunn Palace with Strudel Demonstration
Date: Sunday, May 6, 2018
Time: 9:15 am – 12:15 pm
Duration: 3 hours
Attire: Comfortable clothing and walking shoes
Schonbrunn, the former summer residence of the Imperial family, is considered one of the most beautiful baroque palaces in Europe. The Habsburgs resided here for the better part of the year. Emperor Franz Joseph, who later married the enchanting Sisi and reigned from 1848 to 1916, was born here in 1830. The monarch spent his last years entirely in the palace, which became the property of the new Republic of Austria only two years after his death. Today, the palace is part of UNESCO's cultural heritage due to its historic importance, its unique grounds and its splendid furnishings. Follow our local expert and visit Empress Sisi's former summer residence, then stroll through the park and enjoy the impressive gardens.

The Spanish Riding School Show and Private Tour
Date: Sunday, May 6, 2018
Time: 10:00 am – 3:30 pm
Duration: 5 ½ hours
Attire: Layered clothing, comfortable walking shoes, hat and sunglasses
The Spanish Riding School is the oldest and the last Riding Institute in which the classical art of riding is cultivated in its purest form. You will enjoy a traditional show followed by a private and exclusive tour of the stables.  The tour will end with lunch at famed Café Demel.

Budapest, Hungary

Rendezous with Budapest
Date: Sunday, May 7, 2017
Time: 9:00 am – 1:30 pm
Duration: 4 ½ hours
Attire: Comfortable clothing, walking shoes and sunscreen
During your rendezvous with the beautiful capital, you will see the most interesting sights of the twin-city: Buda-Pest. First stop is the Castle district, where you will explore the most historical area of the capital, and then enjoy a drink with some Hungarian delicacies on the terrace of the charming Fisherman's Bastion.  Afterwards, crossing the River Danube you will arrive back to the Pest side and drive to the Heroes Square, where the country's dramatic history comes to life with the Millennial Monument.   Following Hero's Square, you will visit  the pearl of Budapest, the domed neo-Gothic Parliament rising on the banks of the Danube. The Building is 268 m long, its greatest width is 123 m and the dome is 96 m high. There are 10 courtyards, 27 gates and 29 stairways. The outer walls are adorned with 88 statues depicting Hungarian monarchs, commanders and famous warriors - beautifully illuminated at night, it is truly spectacular. The last action of the tour is a visit and free time at the Grand Market Hall before returning to the Avalon Panorama.

Wine Tasting in the Vineyards of Budapest
Date: Monday, May 7, 2018
Time: 10:00 am – 3:45 pm
Duration: 5 ¾ hours
Attire: Comfortable clothing, walking shoes and sunscreen
As a result of centuries-long viticulture, Etyek has recently joined Hungary's historic wine-districts. The more and more popular wines of Etyek are made from the grapes grown on an area of almost 800 ha. Transfer from Budapest to Etyek takes about 40 minutes.

You will visit the cozy Kálna winery. This family-owned cellar has a 2-hectare territory where they grow mostly white grapes. The winery is famous for its pleasant sparkling wines. You will get an inside tour of the cellar to get an insight to the sparkling wine making traditions in Hungary. Afterwards, you will prepare your own souvenir sparkling wine with the help of the wine-maker himself.

The second stop of the tour is a modern winery only a couple of minutes away. During your visit to the Haraszthy-Vallejo Cellar you will learn more about the history of Etyek wine region, the Hungarian traditions related to and will be introduced to the grape.  You will enjoy a delicious lunch at the Cinnamon restaurant, in a majestical and serene setting. It is a truly unique and relaxing experience.
