Destination

Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas!

Las Vegas, is a city that never disappoints! It delivers fun, breathtaking sights and incredible entertainment around the clock! The Las Vegas Strip cityscape is highlighted by its use of contemporary architecture, lights and wide variety of attractions. Its hotels, casinos, restaurants, residential high-rises, entertainment offerings, and skyline have established the Las Vegas Strip as one of the most popular and iconic tourist destinations in the United States, and the world.  While you’re here, you’ll have a chance to wonder at the skylines of New York and Paris, go on a Venetian gondola ride; and of course, get your camera ready for the delightful dancing waters of the Fountains at Bellagio and the lava-spewing drama of the Mirage Volcano. 

The Las Vegas strip is a playground of entertainment; pull out your wallet and try your luck in one of the dozens of opulent casinos. Outside of the casino, there is something for everyone.  Catch a show featuring big name entertainment, fabulous acrobatic feats or mind-blowing illusions. Enjoy expansive pools to soak up the desert sun or experience the nightlife at a trendy ultra-lounge.

Las Vegas is consistently ranked as one of the world’s top destinations -  luxury hotels and resorts, the endless array of activities and 320 days of sunshine are what make Las Vegas a hot spot.   Everyone knows all about the iconic sights that fill the travel guides. But here are a few interesting tidbits about the desert metropolis.

  • An average of 42,000,000 visitors visit Las Vegas each year.
  • It would take 288 years for one person to spend one night in every hotel room in Las Vegas.
  • The Las Vegas Strip is the brightest place on Earth when looked at from outer space.
  • The Bronze lion outside of the MGM Grand Hotel weighs 50 tons, making it the largest bronze sculpture in the country.
  • Mobster Bugsy Siegel named his casino, The Flamingo, after his showgirl girlfriend, whose long legs garnered her the same name.
  • In some Asian cultures, the number 4 is thought be bad luck. For that reason, some hotels in Vegas have no floors that start with the number 4.
  • There are over 300 weddings per day in Las Vegas, making it the top wedding destination in the US and second only to Istanbul for most number of weddings in a single city.
