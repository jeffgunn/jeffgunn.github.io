#RealPeopleRealNuts

Bringing stories and insight from the U.S. and the world’s most productive peanut and tree nut farming and manufacturing communities, Golden Peanut and Tree Nuts is committed to its people. Coma survival, the everyday struggle of a farmer’s wife, and the process of adopting four siblings are just a few of the journeys these men and women have undergone. Follow their stories with us.

Follow their stories on Instagram and Facebook

Steward of the Land
For farmer Marty McLendon, producing a profitable crop and taking care of the earth that his peanuts and cotton grow in go hand in hand. He worked with agricultural engineers to develop a sustainable irrigation system to water his land that is now used and manufactured all over the world.
My Perfect Storm
After surviving a coma, Golden Peanut and Tree Nuts employee Matt Miller had to start over from the ground up, learning how to cope with a brain injury and navigate school at an age when he expected to be well on his way in a career as a scientist.
Money Grows On Trees
South African farmer Frikkie Nel’s plan to pay for his health care bills and retirement? Growing pecans.
Not for Sissies
Lori Jordan has worked with Golden Peanut and Tree Nuts for 33 years – and she’s learned a thing or two. As a woman in a leadership position in the agricultural industry, her experience has been unique.
Like Father Like Son
Clint White’s great-great grandfather settled in Northern Texas in 1890. Several generations later, Clint and his son Colby are still working the land. But they’ve put their own spin on it: hog training.
The Crazy Eight
Golden Peanut and Tree Nuts employee Brandon Harrell talks about the everyday triumphs and travails of raising four adopted brothers and two teenage daughters.
